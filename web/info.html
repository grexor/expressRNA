News
20. July 2018
Moving fast forward to expressRNA v2, with user sequence data upload. We now have separate interfaces for Libraries (experiment datasets) and Analyses (alternative polyadenylation and RNAmotifs2). The interface is redesigned to be more user friendly, with search+pagination and other niceties. Top right you can suggest ideas and report issues over GitHub :-)

5. January 2018
Yey! Further developments and improvements on the web interface part. Now collaborators can search and browse analysis and libraries. It was about time, since we are adding one new library per week on average! Stay tuned for more.

22. December 2017
The alternative polyadenylation analysis interface is now refreshed, and we added a fold-change interactive graph to explore genes that undergo major proximal/distal switches. An example you can explore is our published TDP-43 KD analysis.

20. December 2017
We are working on the web interface redesign and upgrade, especially the search and browsing of analysis and sequencing libraries. The top menu first icon changed from "Browse" to "Start", since with a single click you can access and browse analysis results.

15. December 2017
The main focus of expressRNA is in the processing of 3'-end targeted sequence data, watch the video to follow our analysis on the showcase of TDP-43 and it's role in APA.
15. November 2017
Exciting times for expressRNA in November, with several new ongoing collaborations and support for Nanopore and PacBio long-reads coming shortly. The long-reads spanning entire transcripts are ideal for the study of alternative polyadenylation and alternative splicing at the same time, and the team at expressRNA is developing a module to do just that. Stay tuned!

12. October 2017
expressRNA is undergoing extensive improvements and updates. Today we also moved to a new server. More news soon.

1. January 2017
The analysis platform is renamed to expressRNA, since the platform combines several software for analysis of post-transcriptional processes, not only on the level of alternative polyadenylation.

25. June 2016
The alternative polyadenylation (APA) platform, apaExpress.org, is now online! In the preceding months, we applied the platform to several datasets and found it useful to share data and results between involved scientists. Now we are making it publicly available to serve the research community.
Involved Researchers
Contributors
Gregor Rot
Gregor is currently the main researcher, developer and coder at expressRNA. Gregor started working on iCLIP and alternative polyadenylation analysis with Tomaž Curk and Jernej Ule in 2009 and obtained his Phd with Christian von Mering from the University of Zurich in 2016. Gregor is currently an Independent Researcher, collaborating with several research laboratories around the world, mostly on computational analysis of post-transcriptional modifications, while keeping eyes open for a relevant PostDoc or Staff Scientist position (contact him over e-mail;).
Tomaž Curk
Tomaž developed the first version of the computational analysis pipeline of 3'-end targeted sequencing data. The data was obtained by applying the wet-lab protocol that Zhen Wang developed in Ule's lab. Tomaž continues to be involved in developing novel analysis methods of RNA-protein binding and post-transcriptional regulation and is Assistant Professor at the Faculty of Computer and Information Science, University of Ljubljana, Slovenia.
João F. Matias Rodrigues
Joao is helping with several ideas and suggestions on computational data analysis and statistics, among others on integrating regulatory signals to RBPs via RNA-maps.
Miha Modic
Miha is working with embryonic stem cells and studies the role of alternative polyadenylation in the context of cell differentiation and pluripotency, among other things. Miha contributed several datasets to expressRNA and suggested several improvements to the platform.
Martina Hallegger
Martina performed the iCLIP experiments of TDP-43 in HEK-293 cells, which provided the crucial dataset to compute RNA-maps of TDP-43, disclosing the regulatory function of the protein in alternative polyadenylation. Martina continues to perform experimental work and use expressRNA for the study of 3'-end processing.
Affiliated Research Groups
Jernej Ule Laboratory, The Francis Crick Institute, London, UK
Jernej Ule lab remains instrumental in guiding research on APA and helping develop all aspects of expressRNA, including RNAmotifs2 analysis software. Jernej holds a joint position between UCL and The Francis Crick Institute, London, UK.
Mark Robinson Laboratory, Institute of Molecular Life Sciences, Zurich, Switzerland
Mark Robinson lab is the main guide and advisor in analysis of long-read Nanopore and PacBio sequence data. There is a new wave of exciting opportunities arising from the long-read sequences spanning entire transcripts that expressRNA is trying to catch.
Christian von Mering Laboratory, Institute of Molecular Life Sciences, Zurich, Switzerland
Christian von Mering lab provided the initial excellent research environment where expressRNA was developed. Several lab members contributed ideas and suggestions.
Brid Ryan Laboratory, National Cancer Institute, Washington, USA
Brid Ryan lab is using expressRNA to perform analysis of alternative polyadenylation in cancer since beginning of 2017. Brid and her team help develop expressRNA greatly by providing ideas and suggestions.
Eran Hornstein Laboratory, Weizmann Institute of Science, Israel
Eran Hornstein lab is using expressRNA to study alternative polyadentlation in several mouse experiments connected with ALS. Eran and his team provide ideas and point out advantages and disadvantages of several expressRNA analysis. This is incredibly valuable information for the team at expressRNA to further improve and develop the platform.
Pietro Fratta Laboratory, UCL London, UK
Pietro Fratta lab is applying expressRNA to libraries of 3'-end targeted and classic RNA-seq. Pietro and his team provide valuable input in terms of identifiying strenghts and weaknesses of expressRNA and help test the platform.
Christian Lehner Laboratory, Institute of Molecular Life Sciences, Zurich, Switzerland
Christian Lehner lab is studying alternative polyadenylation in Drosophila, with a very interesting experimental setup that is challenging in terms of comparative analysis. The research provides lot's of developmental opportunities for expressRNA.

Alternative Polyadenylation
expressRNA post-transcriptional research platform supports Lexogen QuantSeq 3'-end targeted and Nanopore long-reads data for alternative polyadenylation analysis. Research groups and companies are welcome to get in touch to establish new collaborations. Write to us directly expressRNA@gmail.com to start new collaborations.

Post-transcriptional Bioinformatics
We are keen on starting new collaborations on bioinformatics data analysis of post-transcriptional processes. Please feel free to contact us directly over e-mail with suggestions, questions or collaboration initiatives.

Using expressRNA/RNAmotifs2 on your own server
All software used for the analysis is available at the apa, RNAmotifs2 and pybio GitHub repositories and is free for non-commercial use (see Licence).
